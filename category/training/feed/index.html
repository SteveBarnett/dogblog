Roxy!

So, following on from our “Let’s go to Pinelands” thing, we, um, didn’t. Well, we did. But then we didn’t.

We did the flyball there for a while, dipping out after a few weeks. The trainer was lovely, but the other handlers kind of put us off. We looked at them and thought: you’re taking this a little bit too seriously, you know that? And we were worried we would become like them. And, to be honest, we weren’t interested in doing the competition stuff, and it’s all kind of geared towards that/

While were Balling (yo) we spied on the classes. Despite my “needs structure” light complaints before, this pushed it a bit too far. The trainers were shouting at the handlers. It was like watching a drill sergeant and the new recruits. It didn’t look fun for the humans or the dogs. So we decided it was not for us.

A few days after that, I got an SMS from the lovely Newlands lady asking if we’d be interested in more classes. We said hell yes! The vibe is so much nicer. We go to have fun and learn stuff, and we do.

She was falling asleep in the car on the way home.
Normally this is great, but in an hour or so’s time was…

Puppy Class

We’ve opted, for now, for a class in Newlands.
The one we really wanted to go to is Taryn Blyth‘s (she sounds great and has the kind of approach that we concur with), but she’s out in Kommetjie, and so it’s not really practical.

Roxy barely woke up for the trip there, but found a new source of energy once we arrived and especially once the other puppies did. (0_0)!
She was a little bit mental for most of the class, and didn’t do so well on obeying commands and things. She was very excited by all the other dogs.

I think we’ve identified more specifically what the problem is. Whenever we take her to see other dogs it’s playtime. We let the dogs run around while the humans chat. We need to get RoxBox used to the idea that presence of other dogs doesn’t always equal playtime.
This will be difficult!

The other pups and owners are lovely, and the class leader lady is very nice. I found the class a little frustrating, but this is partially my fault at not speaking up.
The class is teaching things like sit, stay, etc. from scratch, but we’ve been doing these at home. (A lot! Her breakfast and dinner kibbles are served as rewards for obeying commands. Roxy has the commands down pat inside, away from distraction. She’s still learning to do these things on walks (pretty good) and in the garden (not so good).)
It seemed that the other owners’ problems were teaching their dog what sit means, whereas ours is teaching Roxy that she must still sit when we say it, even if other dogs are about!

It could very well be the case that the other owners are in the same position as us, and not speaking up for fear of sounding odd / arrogant / boastful. I’m going to be more pro-active next time and talk to the the teacher about this. How do we get Roxy to chill out a bit and obey when there are lots of other doggies around.
