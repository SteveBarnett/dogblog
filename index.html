Brief update

Dog takes on Human stuff,Training 28 September 2011 | Comments Off

Roxy!

So, following on from our “Let’s go to Pinelands” thing, we, um, didn’t. Well, we did. But then we didn’t.

We did the flyball there for a while, dipping out after a few weeks. The trainer was lovely, but the other handlers kind of put us off. We looked at them and thought: you’re taking this a little bit too seriously, you know that? And we were worried we would become like them. And, to be honest, we weren’t interested in doing the competition stuff, and it’s all kind of geared towards that/

While were Balling (yo) we spied on the classes. Despite my “needs structure” light complaints before, this pushed it a bit too far. The trainers were shouting at the handlers. It was like watching a drill sergeant and the new recruits. It didn’t look fun for the humans or the dogs. So we decided it was not for us.

A few days after that, I got an SMS from the lovely Newlands lady asking if we’d be interested in more classes. We said hell yes! The vibe is so much nicer. We go to have fun and learn stuff, and we do.

Getting serious + laughing

Training 5 June 2011 | Comments Off

We went to check out the Pinelands dog school yesterday. Wow, that place is jumping! It was quite a sight to come around the corner to a big school field with a few dozen dogs and handlers. There were a couple of classes running, plus agility stuff going on.
To be honest, it was quite intimidating! It looked so professional and regimented and we felt a bit like yokels bringing our special home school kid: “Yes’m, we done trained her to lick Betsy the cow. She do it goooood!”
The staff / trainers / teachers were absolutely lovely though, and we immediately felt welcome (and they said nice things about Roxy, which always helps!). They were interested and informed, and when the next run of classes start near the end of July we will definitely be there with Roxy to do some “proper” training.
Our previous classes in Newlands were great, but not quite as structured as I wanted. At the moment, it feels like this new place is swinging a lot in the other direction, but it feels good. More obedience / discipline is a good thing. For us as well as Roxy!

I’m excited to start up training things again, but we have a little catching up to do. Heeling requires some work, and we’re going to start doing clicker training. We bought a clicker from the club shop (which has all types of awesome stuff), and it came with the Best Packaging Ever:

No, your eyes do not deceive you: cat in tiara; dog with bow tie; parrot in tux. I don’t think there could be more Winning that this.

Back to school

Puppy Class 2 28 March 2011 | Comments Off

We’ve picked up puppy classes again (after a bit of to-ing and fro-ing and other potential puppy parents dropping out at the last minute) last weekend and it was very groovy.
Roxy enjoys bouncing around and doing stuff, and meeting and playing with new doggies, and we enjoy teaching her new stuff and watching her learn it and get it right.
We’ve hit a bit of a plateau at home: we’re not quite sure what to teach her next, so it’s nice to have the class as a motivation for new stuff.

There were supposed to be four dogs, but two didn’t turn up. Ahem.
It was nice being in a tiny class for the day, though. More attention, doing more stuff.
Next week we’re being joining by a bunch more people who’ve just finished the Introduction to Dog. According to Dog Lady, it’s mostly Labradors. Arf!

One interesting thing came up in chatting to the other humans: a wider world.
There are two ladies who’ve moved here from Joburg, with their (with papers) Alsation.
The dog is lined up for serious training with cool-sounding names like Agility and Attack.
We did some quick googling about this, and it turns out there’s a whole sub-culture of this stuff: not quite the dog show people, but people doing fun stuff with their dogs like flyball (basically dog relay races) and hoop jumping and that kind of thing.
Roxy is very light and agile and loves jumping and hopping on things, so once she’s a bit older (mostly they recommend 1 year old) we’ll definitely be taking her to something like this.
Boing!
